Start
As soon as you can

You'll be the setter on our main production turning cell. Drawing in one hand, bar in the other, getting jobs proved out and running clean — then leaving them running lights-out overnight with confidence.

It's not a button-pressing job. You'll be deciding tooling, working through fixturing, calling tolerances, and flagging anything on a drawing that's going to cost the customer money it doesn't need to. The foreman will back your judgement on the floor.

Most batches are 50–5,000 off in stainless, Inconel, duplex, and aluminium. Some are one-off prototypes that have to be right first time. We'll show you the machine, the cell, and the sort of jobs you'd be on within the first morning — come and look round before you decide.

+ Day-to-day

What you'll actually be doing.

01

Set, prove out, first-off.Tool offsets, work offsets, fixturing decisions. CMM-trace the first-off before you green-light the batch.

02

Run production batches.50 to 5,000 off in stainless, Inconel, duplex, aluminium and brass. Both spindles, both turrets, used properly.

03

Bar-feed setup.Hydrafeed Rota-Rack loading, bar prep, parting and pickoff. Get it set so it runs unmanned overnight.

04

In-process inspection.Micrometers, bore gauges, vernier, comparator. Spot drift before the batch goes off-tolerance.

05

Edit programs at the machine.Fanuc control. Small program tweaks, speed and feed adjustments, tool path corrections. Programmers do the heavy CAM.

06

Flag DFM issues early.If a drawing's going to cost the customer money for no good reason, you tell the foreman before the bar gets loaded.

07

Hand-off cleanly.Batch paperwork, inspection records, tooling notes for the next setup. ISO 9001:2015 quality system, properly logged.

08

Lights-out prep.Tool monitoring set, coolant topped, swarf cleared, bar loaded. End-of-shift confidence the machine will still be cutting good parts at 6am.

+ What you'll bring

The kit-list, honest.

01 + Essential 3+ years setting production CNC lathes Twin-spindle experience preferred
02 + Essential Fanuc control, comfortable editing at the machine Small program tweaks, not full CAM
03 + Essential Drawing literate — GD&T, tolerance stacks Including aerospace-style drawings
04 + Nice to have Citizen, Miyano or similar bar-fed experience We'll cross-train if you're solid elsewhere
05 + Nice to have Exotic alloys — Inconel, duplex, titanium Most of our work runs in them
06 + Attitude Owns the batch from set to sign-off Asks before guessing. Flags before scrapping.

+ Questions

Things people ask about this role.

When could I start?

As soon as you're free. We'll work with whatever notice period you've got — there's no rush to drop your current employer in it.

What if I've not run a Citizen Miyano before?

That's fine. If you're a confident setter on twin-spindle or sliding-head kit from elsewhere, we'll cross-train you on the Citizen. Expect a couple of weeks alongside the current setter before you're on your own.

Are the hours fixed?

The day shift is the day shift, but the start and finish either side of the half-hour lunch can flex within reason. School run, hospital appointments, the things life throws at you — we'd rather you said and worked around it than hid it.

What's the dress code on the floor?

Workwear and safety boots, both supplied. Hearing and eye protection where the signs say. Long sleeves around the machines. Nothing dangling.

Can I come and look round before I apply?

Yes. Drop us a line on the form or by email and we'll set a time. Half an hour around the machines, half an hour with a brew. No pressure to apply after.
