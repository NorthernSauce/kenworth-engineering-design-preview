+ Geometry costs you the price

On small parts, fixed-head turning costs you the price.

Small-diameter parts on a fixed-head lathe deflect under the cut. Tolerance pushes out. Cycle time burns on every unit. On thousands of parts, that geometry becomes your unit cost.

Sliding head supports the bar in a guide bush right at the cut. Sub-10 micron, every unit, at production speed.

+ Cost geometry

Where sliding head wins on cost.

Under 22mm diameter, above a few hundred parts, sliding head almost always wins on unit cost. The guide bush holds the bar at the cut, so cycle time drops and tolerance holds.

We'll tell you at quote stage whether your part falls inside that band.
